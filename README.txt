
# Clinical Trials for Evidence-Based Medicine in Spanish (CT-EBM-SP) Corpus


## Documents

The CT-EBM-SP corpus version 1 gathers 1200 texts about clinical trial studies:

- 500 abstracts from journals published under a Creative Commons license, e.g. available in PubMed (https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) or the Scientific Electronic Library Online - SciELO (http://www.scielo.org) 
- 700 clinical trials announcements published in the European Clinical Trials Register (www.clinicaltrialsregister.eu) and Repositorio Español de Estudios Clínicos (https://reec.aemps.es/reec/public/web.html)


## Folders and corpus splits

We distribute the files with the same splits used in the experiments conducted and reported in the article (see reference below):

- Train: training set, made up of 720 documents (60% of the corpus):
    train/abstracts: 300 texts
    train/eudract: 420 texts
- Dev: development (or validation) set, made up of 240 documents (20% of the corpus):
    dev/abstracts: 100 texts
    dev/eudract: 140 texts
- Test: development (or validation) set, made up of 240 documents (20% of the corpus):
    test/abstracts: 100 texts
    test/eudract: 140 texts

## Corpus format

The text files are released in plain text (.txt) with UTF-8 encoding.
The annotation files are released in BRAT format (.ann), also in UTF-8 encoding. 


## BRAT configuration files

The files "visual.conf" and "annotation.conf" are used in BRAT to highlight the entities marked according to a color scheme.
Place these files in the directory corresponding to the base directory of the collection (e.g. brat-v1.3_Crunchy_Frog/data/EBM-CT-ES/).
